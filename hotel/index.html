THINGS TO DO IN BANGALORE

Savandurga, Asia's one of largest monolithic hills, is a popular destination for trekking and rock climbing. Enjoy stunning panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys. Savandyrga also has several ancient temples and historic structures.

Bangalore Palace was built in 1887 by Chamaraja Wodeyar and his descendants. The palace, created in Tudor-style architecture, resembles Windsor Castle in England. You can tour them to explore its stunning interiors, royal artifacts, and beautiful paintings.

Hebbal Lake is a scenic freshwater lake in the northern part of Bangalore. It is home to various species of birds, including the spot-billed pelican and the black-crowned night heron. Enjoy a peaceful walk around the lake, watch the sunset, and indulge in bird watching.

ISKCON Temple in Bangalore is a spiritual hub for devotees of Lord Krishna. It has a museum, auditorium, lecture hall, bookshop, and gift shop. You can participate in daily aarti, attend spiritual discourses, and enjoy the delicious prasad.

Kunti Betta is a hillock known for trekking and camping. People connect the hill with the story of Mahabharata. It has a temple dedicated to the mother of the Pandava Kunti. Trek to the hilltop to enjoy the panorama.

Another iconic breakfast place in Bangalore, Vidyarthi Bhavan, is known for its crispy masala dosa, served with butter and chutney. The restaurant has been around since 1943 and is a favourite among locals and tourists.

Brahmin's Coffee Bar is an iconic spot primarily known for its idli & chutney and crunchy yet soft vada. The place has an old-school charm with no sitting place with only standing options.

The Black Rabbit is a gastropub serving various cuisines, including Indian, Asian, and European. The restaurant is known for its craft beers, cocktails, and live music performances.

Karavalli is a popular fine-dining restaurant that serves coastal cuisine from the four southern states of India. The restaurant is known for its seafood, especially the Mangalorean fish curry and prawn ghee roast.

MTR is an iconic restaurant in Bangalore that has been serving delicious South Indian food since 1924. The restaurant is famous for its masala dosa, idli, vada, and filter coffee.
